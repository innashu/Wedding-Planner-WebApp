## Part 1: SF City Hall Marriage License & Ceremony

### Appointments Needed

You need **two separate appointments** (can be same day):
1. Marriage License appointment
2. Civil Ceremony appointment

**Booking:** [SF County Clerk Online Portal](https://www.sf.gov/get-marriage-license)

**Pro tip:** Book ceremony appointment FIRST (they fill up fast), then book license appointment second. Both can be scheduled for November 9, 2026.

---

### Marriage License Appointment Checklist

**Both parties must appear together.**

#### What to Bring:
- [ ] Unexpired government-issued photo ID (for each person)
  - Passport, driver's license, state ID, or military ID
- [ ] If ID doesn't show full legal name: certified birth certificate or Social Security card
- [ ] Payment for license fee (~$100-120, check current fees)

#### Choose License Type:
| Type | Witness Required? | Public Record? | Requirements |
|------|------------------|----------------|--------------|
| **Public** | Yes (1 witness) | Yes | Standard |
| **Confidential** | No | No | Must be cohabiting |

#### Important Notes:
- License is valid for 90 days from issuance
- Cannot reschedule - must cancel and rebook if needed
- No refunds available
- **CRITICAL:** Select your new married name on the license application (required for name change)

---

### Civil Ceremony Appointment Checklist

#### What to Bring:
- [ ] Valid photo ID (for each person)
- [ ] **Original** marriage license (no photocopies)
- [ ] 1 witness (for public license only)
  - Must be 18+ years old
  - Must be present for entire ceremony
  - Will sign the marriage license
  - Photographer can serve as witness; officiant cannot
- [ ] Rings (if exchanging)

#### Guest Limit:
- Maximum **6 guests** permitted at civil ceremonies

#### Day-Of Tips:
- Arrive 15 minutes before appointment
- Have witness with you at check-in (can be called immediately)
- Ceremony is brief (~5-10 minutes)

## Part 2: Name Change After Marriage

### Critical First Step

**On the marriage license application:** Select your new married name. This is legally required to change your name via marriage. If skipped, you'll need a court order.

---

### Order of Operations (Must Follow This Sequence)

#### Step 1: Obtain Certified Marriage Certificates

- [ ] Request multiple certified copies at ceremony or order from County Clerk
- [ ] Recommended: Get 5-10 copies (needed for each agency)
- [ ] Cost: ~$15-20 per certified copy

---

#### Step 2: Social Security Administration / SSA (DO THIS FIRST)

**What is SSA?** The Social Security Administration is the U.S. government agency that manages Social Security numbers and cards.

**Why first?** The DMV verifies your info against SSA records. If SSA isn't updated, DMV will deny your application.

**Location:** [Find your local SSA office](https://www.ssa.gov/locator/)

##### What to Bring:
- [ ] Original or certified marriage certificate
- [ ] Valid photo ID (current driver's license or passport)
- [ ] Completed [Form SS-5](https://www.ssa.gov/forms/ss-5.pdf) (Application for Social Security Card)

##### Process:
- [ ] Submit in person or by mail
- **Cost:** Free
- **Processing time:** ~2 weeks for new card
- **Important:** Wait 48 hours after SSA processes before going to DMV

---

#### Step 3: California DMV (DO THIS SECOND)

**Website:** [DMV.ca.gov](https://www.dmv.ca.gov)

##### What to Bring:
- [ ] New Social Security card OR SSA receipt/confirmation
- [ ] Current driver's license
- [ ] Certified marriage certificate
- [ ] Proof of current address (utility bill, bank statement, etc.)

##### Process:
- [ ] Complete online application at DMV.ca.gov
- [ ] Schedule appointment
- [ ] Visit DMV in person

##### While at DMV, Also Update:
- [ ] Voter registration
- [ ] Vehicle title (if applicable)
- [ ] Vehicle registration (if applicable)

##### REAL ID Tip:
If planning to get REAL ID, bring additional required documents to update name and upgrade in one visit.

---

#### Step 4: Passport

**For existing passport:**
- [ ] Complete [Form DS-82](https://travel.state.gov) (renewal by mail) if passport is less than 15 years old
- OR Complete [Form DS-11](https://travel.state.gov) (new application) if older

##### What to Submit:
- [ ] Current passport
- [ ] Certified marriage certificate
- [ ] Passport photo
- [ ] Applicable fee

**Timing:** Do this before honeymoon if traveling internationally with new name, OR travel with current passport and update after.
